Bee Friendly Plants

The Great British Bee is one of the most important players in British agriculture, pollinating a third of everything we eat and more generally keeping the planet’s ecosystem sustained.

However, bees are in decline all over the world due to man-made changes in their habitats through new farming techniques and urbanisation; since 1945, 97% of wildflower meadows in the UK have disappeared. This is why it is so important that the garden owners of Britain do their bit for bees, and it really is as simple as planting.

Here is a list of six of the best plants for bees, and other pollinators too:

Lavender

Bees see the colour purple more clearly than any other so any purple plant will be a hit. Lavender, and English Lavender in particular, seems to be a firm favourite.

Rose Campion

This long-flowering perennial keeps the bees occupied for the whole summer.

Echinacea

Bees particularly love cone flowers; they’re are easy to access and help produce some of the most delicious honey.

Bluebeard (Caryopteris)

This small perennial, also purplish blue in colour, is very popular with butterflies too.

Helenium

These late-summer perennials not only look great late into the summer but bees and butterflies are particularly fond of them too.

Bergamot

Also known as Bee Balm, this relatively low-maintenance perennial looks great all the way through to early autumn.
