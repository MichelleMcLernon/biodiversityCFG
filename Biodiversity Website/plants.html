Plants

The Great British Bee is one of the most important players in British agriculture, pollinating a third of everything we eat and more generally keeping the planet’s ecosystem sustained. However, bees are in decline all over the world due to man-made changes in their habitats through new farming techniques and urbanisation; since 1945, 97% of wildflower meadows in the UK have disappeared. This is why it is so important that the garden owners of Britain do their bit for bees, and it really is as simple as planting. Here’s a list of eight of the best plants for bees, and other pollinators too.

English Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia)

Bees see the colour purple more clearly than any other so any purple plant will be a hit. Lavender, and English Lavender in particular, seems to be a firm favourite.

Bluebeard (Caryopteris x clandonensis)

This small perennial, also purplish blue in colour, is very popular with butterflies too.

Echinaceas

Bees particularly love cone flowers; they’re are easy to access and help produce some of the most delicious honey.
